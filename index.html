The problem

Finance is a fragmented
database problem.

Every participant runs its own ledger. Trillions sit frozen for 48 hours. Most of what operations teams do is reconcile identical records held separately.

Today — 5 intermediaries
Brokerage → Clearinghouse → Custodian → CCP → Settlement Agent
T+2 delay · $5B+ annual fails cost · ~80% of ops is reconciliation
ChainSettle — 1 shared ledger
Trade submitted → Validated → Atomic DvP → Finalized
T+0 · ~4 seconds · zero reconciliation · immutable audit trail
The platform

Four components.
One settlement layer.

Each module replaces a distinct intermediary function. Together they form a complete, programmable settlement infrastructure.

01 / LEDGER
Shared settlement ledger
A permissioned blockchain where every participant sees the same single truth. Trade execution, ownership transfer, and finality recorded once — visible to all counterparties simultaneously.
Hyperledger Besu · DAML · Canton Network compatible
02 / CONTRACTS
Smart contract engine
Delivery-versus-payment executed atomically: either both legs settle or both revert. Margin calls, collateral release, accrued interest — fully automated, fully auditable. No phone calls.
Solidity · Formal verification · Pre-flight KYC/AML validation
03 / COLLATERAL
Real-time collateral engine
Tokenized T-bills, money market funds, and stablecoins locked and released automatically as positions move. Haircuts applied by smart contract. Live margin health for every counterparty.
T-Bill · MMF · USDC / USDT · Auto-haircut · Real-time margining
04 / COMPLIANCE
Compliance & regulator layer
KYC and AML baked into the transaction layer, not bolted on. Every event is cryptographically signed and immutable. Regulators get a live, read-only audit view. 100% coverage, zero effort.
SEC / FINRA read-only portal · Immutable on-chain audit log · KYC API

Roadmap

Full lifecycle disruption, one phase at a time.

Each phase eliminates another class of intermediary. Phase I is the wedge — the highest-volume, most pain-filled function in institutional markets.

Phase I
Settlement & Collateral
Eliminates clearinghouses, settlement agents, and reconciliation operations entirely.
MVP NOW
Phase II
Issuance & Transfer Agency
Eliminates transfer agents, DTCC bookentry, and IPO underwriter ledgers.
Phase III
Corporate Actions
Eliminates corporate action processors, proxy agents, and dividend administrators.
Phase IV
Full Exchange & Clearing
Eliminates traditional exchanges, CCPs, and the market maker intermediation layer.
